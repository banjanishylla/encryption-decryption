How Caesar Cipher works
Each letter in the plaintext is shifted by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet. With shift 3: A→D, B→E, Z→C. Numbers, spaces, and punctuation are left unchanged. To decrypt, simply shift in the opposite direction (or shift by 26 − n). Julius Caesar reportedly used a shift of 3 for military communications.
